Data Limitations and Mitigation Strategy
Project Title: Analyzing the Drivers of Gaming Fatigue
Analyst: Danish Khan
Phase: Prepare

1. Platform Bias (The PC vs. Console Divide)
- Limitation: The `steam_store_games.csv` dataset exclusively represents the PC gaming market. It does not account for console exclusives, console-specific player behaviors, or the mobile gaming market.
- Mitigation: When analyzing genre saturation and playtime, conclusions will be explicitly scoped to the PC/Steam ecosystem. The multi-platform `Video_Game_Sales_and_Ratings.csv` dataset will be used to cross-reference and validate broader industry trends.

2. The Digital Transition Gap
- Limitation: Historical global sales data notoriously underreports digital downloads. Because the industry shifted heavily from physical to digital between 2010 and 2020, sales numbers for recent games in the `Video_Game_Sales_and_Ratings.csv` file may be artificially lower than their true market penetration.
- Mitigation: Sales volume will be treated as a directional indicator of a game's popularity rather than an absolute financial metric. The "Market Overwhelm" factor will primarily be measured by the sheer volume of new game releases rather than raw sales numbers.

3. Timeframe Discrepancies
- Limitation: The Steam dataset captures data up to late 2019. It effectively captures the initial "explosion" of games post-2015 (Steam Direct) but misses the pandemic-induced gaming boom of 2020-2022 and the most recent wave of live-service fatigue.
- Mitigation: The analysis will heavily focus on the critical transition period between the "Golden Era" (2000-2015) and the start of the modern saturated era (2015-2019). The Sales/Ratings dataset (which extends to 2023) will be utilized to bridge the gap and extend sentiment analysis into the current decade.

4. Playtime Metric Flaws
- Limitation: On Steam, the `average_playtime` metric can be heavily skewed by a small percentage of players leaving games running idle or utilizing third-party programs to farm Steam Trading Cards.
- Mitigation: The analysis will prioritize `median_playtime` over `average_playtime` during the Process phase. The median is far more robust against these extreme outliers and better represents the typical player's actual time investment.

5. Lack of Temporal Retention Data
- Limitation: The datasets provide total playtime, but lack longitudinal data (e.g., how many players dropped off after week 1, week 2, etc.). We cannot definitively prove exactly *when* a player felt fatigued during a specific game, only the final outcome of their total engagement.
- Mitigation: We will use the gap between Critic Scores (which generally reflect day-one reception) and User Scores (which reflect long-term consumer sentiment) as a proxy for player burnout over time.
