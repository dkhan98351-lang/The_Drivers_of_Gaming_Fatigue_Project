# Project Brief: Analyzing the Drivers of Gaming Fatigue
**Analyst:** Danish Khan

---

## 1. Executive Summary
The video game industry has experienced unprecedented growth, transitioning from curated, finite releases to a saturated market dominated by live-service models and continuous content delivery. While consumer choice has expanded, this shift has introduced a documented phenomenon of "gaming fatigue." Players increasingly report backlog anxiety, shortened engagement periods, and burnout. This project analyzes historical sales, release volumes, genre distributions, and player engagement metrics to quantify the drivers of this fatigue, with a specific focus on the industry paradigm shift following 2015.

## 2. Business Objective
To provide data-driven insights into how market saturation, game length, and release frequency impact long-term player retention and sentiment. The findings will serve to help developers optimize game design and release strategies, while enabling consumers to make informed purchasing decisions that mitigate burnout.

## 3. Scope and Methodology
* **Data Sources:** * Steam Store Games Dataset (Release dates, genres, playtime metrics, categories).
    * Video Game Sales and Ratings Dataset (Historical global sales, critic scores, user scores).
* **Tech Stack:** * **Data Storage & Extraction:** SQL
    * **Data Cleaning & Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA):** Python (Pandas, NumPy)
    * **Data Modeling & Visualization:** Power BI

## 4. Key Research Questions
1.  **Market Overwhelm:** How has the exponential increase in yearly game releases impacted average player engagement and per-title completion rates?
2.  **The Time Investment Factor:** Is there a statistically significant correlation between inflated average playtimes (indicative of live-service or open-world models) and a degradation in user or critic sentiment over time?
3.  **Genre Saturation Analysis:** Which specific gaming genres exhibit the highest disparity between release volume/sales and actual median playtime, suggesting acute player burnout?
4.  **Platform Discrepancies:** Do certain gaming platforms demonstrate a higher propensity for fatigue indicators (e.g., lower playtime rates relative to library size) compared to others?
5.  **The Generational Shift (2000-2015 vs. Modern Era):** How do macro-level metrics from the "golden era" (2000–2015) contrast with the post-2015 landscape? Do these systemic shifts empirically explain the phenomenon of "veteran player fatigue"?

## 5. Expected Deliverables
* Fully documented Python/Pandas data cleaning scripts.
* A relational data model structured for analytical querying via SQL.
* An interactive Power BI dashboard highlighting key fatigue indicators and generational trends.
* A final executive presentation summarizing actionable insights.
